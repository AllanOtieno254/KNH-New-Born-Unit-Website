Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to help you understand care processes, visiting policies, and what to expect during your baby’s stay.

We understand that having a baby in the neonatal unit can feel overwhelming. This page answers the most common questions parents ask. If you need further clarification, our care team is always available.

General Questions

The cost of neonatal care depends on your baby’s condition, length of stay, and level of medical support required. Our billing team will explain all charges clearly and help you understand payment options or insurance coverage.

Parents are encouraged to visit daily. Visiting hours may vary depending on medical procedures, infection control policies, and your baby’s condition.

Nurses provide regular updates during ward rounds and whenever there is a change in your baby’s condition. Parents are welcome to ask questions and request clarification at any time.

Yes. Mothers are encouraged to provide expressed breast milk. Our team will guide you on proper storage, labeling, and handling to ensure safety.

Discharge depends on your baby’s ability to feed, breathe independently, maintain body temperature, and gain weight consistently. Parents receive full discharge education beforehand.

Small personal items such as baby clothes or blankets may be allowed after approval. For infection control reasons, some items may be restricted.

Still Have Questions?

Our neonatal care team understands that every family’s situation is unique. If you need further clarification, we encourage you to speak directly with your nurse or doctor.

Available Support:

  • Direct nurse communication
  • Doctor consultation during rounds
  • Parent education sessions
  • Discharge planning meetings

You Are Not Alone

Our team is here to support you emotionally and medically throughout your baby’s journey. Asking questions is encouraged — your involvement plays a vital role in your baby’s care.
