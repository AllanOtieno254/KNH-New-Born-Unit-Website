Glossary: Terms Parents Should Know

CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) is a method used to help premature babies breathe more easily. It delivers continuous air pressure to keep the baby’s lungs inflated and prevent collapse. CPAP is non-invasive and helps babies maintain oxygen levels without a breathing tube.

Phototherapy uses special blue light to reduce high bilirubin levels (jaundice) in newborns. The light helps break down bilirubin in the skin safely.

An incubator is a special enclosed crib that keeps premature babies warm, protects them from infection, and monitors vital signs.

The Apgar Score evaluates a newborn’s health at 1 and 5 minutes after birth. It checks Appearance, Pulse, Grimace, Activity, and Respiration.

An NG (Nasogastric) Tube is a small tube inserted through the nose into the stomach for feeding babies who cannot suckle yet.

Jaundice is a yellowing of the skin and eyes caused by high bilirubin levels. Common in newborns, it is often treated with phototherapy.

Oxygen therapy provides extra oxygen to babies who have trouble breathing or maintaining oxygen levels in the blood.

Premature refers to babies born before 37 weeks of gestation. They often require special care in the NICU.

Respiratory distress occurs when a baby has difficulty breathing, often due to immature lungs.

Sepsis is a serious infection in the bloodstream that requires prompt medical treatment.
