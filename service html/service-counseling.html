Parental Counseling Services

Compassionate emotional, psychological, and practical support for parents navigating the challenges of neonatal hospitalization.

Our Programs

Overview

Having a newborn in the NBU can be overwhelming—filled with uncertainty, fear, and emotional strain. Our counseling program is designed to support parents throughout this journey by creating a safe, non-judgmental space for expression, healing, and understanding. We walk with families through every phase of hospitalization, offering daily updates, emotional coping strategies, crisis support, parenting skills, and guidance for transitioning home.

Whether you are experiencing anxiety, grief, stress, spiritual questions, or simply need someone to listen, our counselors ensure that no parent faces the NBU journey alone.

Counseling Programs

  • Daily bedside support: Gentle, compassionate communication to help families understand clinical updates and make informed decisions.
  • Psychosocial assessments: Screening for emotional strain, trauma, depression, family stress, or other psychological challenges that may require follow-up support.
  • One-to-one counseling: Personalized sessions addressing grief, fear, coping difficulties, guilt, postpartum emotional changes, and family conflict.
  • Family & couple counseling: Helping caregivers communicate better, share responsibilities, and maintain healthy relationships during difficult periods.
  • Peer & group support sessions: Guided discussions that connect parents facing similar experiences, reducing isolation and building community.
  • Crisis intervention: Immediate emotional support for parents coping with sudden clinical deterioration, emergencies, or unexpected diagnoses.
  • Bereavement counseling: Sensitive guidance and follow-up support for families experiencing loss, including grief processing and memorial planning.
  • Discharge and home-care transition: Preparing parents emotionally and practically for caring for their newborn at home.

Counseling Team

Our multidisciplinary team works together to ensure families receive holistic emotional and practical support.

Clinical Psychologist

Provides mental health assessments, trauma support, therapeutic counseling, and referrals for specialized psychiatric services when needed.

Social Workers

Assists with social and financial challenges, family support linkages, NHIF/insurance guidance, documentation, safeguarding concerns, and resource referrals.

FAQs — Counseling

How do I arrange a counseling session?

You can request a counselor through your bedside nurse at any time. A team member will be assigned to you based on urgency and availability.

Is counseling confidential?

Yes. All counseling conversations are confidential except in cases where there are safety concerns that must be reported for legal or clinical reasons.

Does counseling cost extra?

No. Counseling services at the NBU are provided at no additional cost to families.

Can both parents attend the sessions?

Absolutely. Sessions can be arranged for mothers, fathers, extended caregivers, or jointly as a family, depending on preference.
