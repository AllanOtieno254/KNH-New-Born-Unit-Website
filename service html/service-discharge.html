Discharge Planning

Safe transition from hospital to home — tailored discharge plans, parent education, and coordinated support.

Discharge Process

Overview

Discharge planning begins early during admission to help parents confidently take their baby home. Our multidisciplinary team ensures your newborn is medically ready while preparing you emotionally and practically.

Medical Readiness

Your baby must maintain temperature, feed well, breathe comfortably, and show steady progress.

Parent Readiness

Caregivers are trained on feeding, medicines, hygiene, warmth, and recognizing danger signs.

Home Readiness

We help ensure the home environment is safe, clean, and supportive for your baby.

Discharge Process

1. Final Medical Review

A full check by the consultant, nurse, and clinical team confirming stability and readiness.

2. Parent Education

Hands-on teaching on feeding, temperature regulation, safe sleep, medications, hygiene, and danger signs.

3. Documentation

Discharge summary, immunization booklet updates, follow-up dates, referral letters, and prescriptions.

4. Follow-up Planning

Scheduling neonatal clinic visits, developmental assessments, and referrals to local clinics.

Homecare Instructions

Your baby’s wellbeing at home depends on close monitoring and consistent routines. Key areas include:

Feeding & Nutrition

Follow the feeding schedule and watch for poor sucking, excessive sleepiness, or vomiting.

Warmth & Temperature

Maintain skin-to-skin, avoid cold rooms, and check for fever or low temperature.

Hygiene & Cleanliness

Ensure clean hands, sterilized feeding items, and safe cord care.

Danger Signs

Difficulty breathing, convulsions, refusal to feed, high fever — seek emergency care immediately.

Additional care includes safe sleeping position, vaccination adherence, correct medicine dosing, and ensuring a smoke-free environment.

Community & Follow-up

After discharge, we stay connected with you through scheduled hospital visits and community support systems. These follow-up channels ensure continued growth monitoring, vaccination completion, and emotional support.

  • KNH Neonatal Follow-up Clinic — growth & developmental review
  • Referral to local clinics for routine immunizations & weighing
  • Community health volunteers for home follow-up
  • Hotline support for urgent concerns

  • I know my follow-up dates
  • I have discussed any financial or social concerns

FAQs — Discharge

When is my baby ready for discharge?

When breathing is stable, feeding consistently, able to maintain temperature, and medically cleared.

What should I prepare at home?

Clean sleeping area, proper bedding, warmth, clean clothes, and follow-up plan.

Can I still breastfeed exclusively at home?

Yes! Your team will guide you on frequency, positioning, and supplementary needs if required.

What if I notice something unusual?

Use the hotline or go to the emergency department immediately. Trust your instincts.

Will someone follow up on my baby?

Yes — the clinic review and community health volunteers ensure continued monitoring.
