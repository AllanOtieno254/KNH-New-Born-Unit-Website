Feeding & Lactation Support

Comprehensive breastfeeding support, milk expression guidance, and special feeding plans for preterm and sick infants.

Our Services Resources

Why Specialized Feeding Support Matters

Premature and ill newborns often struggle with feeding. Our lactation service helps families establish and maintain milk supply, teaches safe expression and storage, and creates individualized feeding plans that balance nutrition and medical needs.

Our Lactation Services

  • One-to-one lactation consultations
  • Instruction on hand expression and pump use
  • Breastmilk storage & safety guidance
  • Assistance with direct breastfeeding when baby is ready
  • Feeding plans for babies with special needs

Pumps & Milk Handling

The unit provides guidance on hospital-grade pumps and proper milk storage. We advise on labeling, refrigerated storage durations, and safe thawing practices.

Lactation Team

Priya — Lactation Consultant

Practical Steps & Tips

  1. Begin skin-to-skin contact when possible.
  2. Express early and frequently (8–12 times/day) if baby is not feeding directly.
  3. Use a hospital-grade pump when advised by staff.
  4. Label milk with date/time and baby’s name—follow storage recommendations.
