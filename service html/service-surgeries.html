Neonatal Surgeries

Specialized surgical care for newborns — from congenital anomalies to emergency neonatal procedures.

Surgical Team Referral Process

Scope of Neonatal Surgery

Our pediatric surgery team treats congenital anomalies (e.g., intestinal atresia, diaphragmatic hernia), emergency neonatal surgical conditions (e.g., necrotizing enterocolitis), and performs corrective procedures when clinically indicated. We coordinate closely with neonatology for perioperative stabilization and postoperative care.

Conditions Treated

  • Congenital intestinal obstruction
  • Necrotizing enterocolitis requiring surgery
  • Congenital diaphragmatic hernia (with neonatal surgical repair)
  • Congenital anomalies requiring staged surgeries

Perioperative Care

Preoperative stabilization in NICU, careful anesthetic planning, sterile procedures in the pediatric operating theater, followed by NICU recovery and specialized nursing.

  1. Stabilization and imaging
  2. Informed consent with parents
  3. Operation & immediate postoperative care
  4. Longer term follow-up and developmental checks

Referral & Emergency Process

For surgical referrals, submit the clinical summary, imaging, and neonatal vitals. For emergency cases, contact the pediatric surgery on-call number and bring necessary referral documents if time allows.

Emergency surgical contact: +254 700 000 004

FAQs — Neonatal Surgery

Is surgery safe for very low birth weight babies?

Risks are higher, but surgery is sometimes lifesaving; multidisciplinary planning optimizes outcomes.

How long is recovery?

Depends on procedure and baby’s baseline health—expect extended hospitalization for complex cases.
