Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC)

Skin-to-skin contact to support thermal regulation, breastfeeding, and bonding for preterm and low birth weight infants.

Learn Benefits How to Practice

What is KMC?

Kangaroo Mother Care is a method of care in which the baby is held skin-to-skin against the parent's chest for extended periods. It helps control body temperature, encourages breastfeeding, and fosters attachment between parent and infant.

Clinical Benefits

  • Better thermal stability and reduced hypothermia
  • Improved breastfeeding initiation and duration
  • Reduced infection rates
  • Improved weight gain and growth
  • Shorter hospital stays in some cases

How to Practise KMC

  1. Find a comfortable chair or bed—parent removes top layers so baby is skin-to-skin.
  2. Position baby upright between breasts or over chest, head turned to side to maintain airway.
  3. Cover baby with a blanket to maintain warmth, keep position for extended periods (as tolerated).
  4. Health staff will teach safe positioning, feeding cues, and monitoring.

Support & Training

Our lactation consultants and nurses run daily KMC education sessions and support parents with practical tips and demonstrations.

Practical Tips for Parents

  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing.
  • Bring pillows for comfort during long sessions.
  • Keep a small blanket handy to cover baby while skin-to-skin.
  • Ask the nurse for guidance on hand hygiene and safe sleep rules.

FAQ — KMC

How long should KMC sessions last?

Longer durations are better—aim for several hours a day as tolerated. The team will guide on realistic goals.

Can fathers practice KMC?

Yes—fathers and other caregivers can perform KMC when trained and when clinical condition allows.
