High Dependency Care (HDU)

Enhanced monitoring and support when a baby needs close observation but not full intensive care.

Meet the HDU Team Parent Guidelines

Overview

HDU provides an intermediate level of care for babies who are stable but need more frequent monitoring than a standard ward. This includes infants transitioning from the NICU or those requiring short-term oxygen therapy, intravenous fluids, or more frequent observations.

HDU Services

Continuous vital sign monitoring: Close observation by trained staff.
Oxygen therapy (low-flow): For mild respiratory support.
Special feeding support: Tube feeding or NG feeds while oral feeding is established.
Medication management: IV or oral medications administered under close supervision.

Patient Flow

Entry to HDU occurs by transfer from NICU or direct admission from the neonatal ward. Patients receive hourly or more frequent observations and individualized nursing care plans. Discharge from HDU occurs when monitoring frequency reduces and the infant can maintain stability on their own.

  1. Assessment & baseline labs
  2. Tailored monitoring plan
  3. Daily multidisciplinary rounds
  4. Discharge planning & outpatient follow-up

Guidelines for Parents

Visiting is usually allowed for parents. Other visitors may be limited. The staff will teach parents how to participate in feeding and basic care before discharge.

  • Keep your hands clean—use hand sanitizer on entry and exit.
  • Keep questions written down for daily rounds.
  • Wear any protective gear requested by staff.

FAQs — HDU

Is parental presence allowed 24/7?

Parents are prioritized, but overnight access is based on space and patient safety; discuss with the unit coordinator.

How long does HDU care last?

Depends on the condition—often shorter than NICU stays. The team will provide regular updates.
